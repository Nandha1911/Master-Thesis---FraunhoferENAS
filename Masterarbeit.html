Project Overview

🔬

The Challenge

Predicting delamination and damage in power semiconductor components under thermal stress is expensive and time-consuming. Traditional experimental approaches require extensive physical testing.

⚡

The Solution

Develop a physics-informed ML framework combining Abaqus finite element simulations with PyTorch-based neural networks and L-BFGS-B constrained optimization for parameter identification.

🎯

The Impact

Achieved >90% prediction accuracy with 6 non-linear damage models evaluated. Framework eliminates extensive experimental testing while maintaining scientific rigor.

Key Objectives

  • Abaqus Modeling: Create 3D thermo-mechanical FEM simulations of power electronic components under thermal cycling (-40°C to 125°C)
  • Damage Models: Compare and evaluate 6 linear and non-linear damage accumulation models (Miner, Hashin-Rotem, Pavlou, Batsoulas, TS)
  • PIML Framework: Develop physics-informed ML for parameter identification using PyTorch and L-BFGS-B optimization
  • Validation: Correlate simulation predictions with experimental data from SAM and PIRT characterization
  • Reliability: Enable digital twin concepts for predictive maintenance and cost reduction

Experimental Reference Data

Material & Testing Setup

SAC (Sn-Ag-Cu) solder is used as the die-attach material in power electronics due to its superior thermal and electrical properties. Damage was experimentally evaluated using Scanning Acoustic Microscopy (SAM) and Pulsed Infrared Thermography (PIRT) after passive thermal cycling tests.

Technical Architecture

Three-Layer Framework

The thesis implements an integrated approach combining finite element analysis, damage modeling, and machine learning optimization:

  1. Abaqus FEM Simulation: Thermo-mechanical analysis with creep modeling
  2. Damage Accumulation Models: 6 non-linear models compared and evaluated
  3. Physics-Informed ML Optimization: PyTorch + L-BFGS-B for parameter calibration

1. Finite Element Modeling in Abaqus

Fig 5: Mesh Discretization
3D mesh with local refinement at corners and interfaces
Fig 6: Mesh Convergence
Convergence study: optimal mesh size = 0.2484 mm
Fig 7: Material Layers
Side view with material definitions and die-attach layer
Fig 8: Boundary Conditions
Symmetry constraints and thermal cycling setup
  • Geometry: 3D electronic component model with all material layers
  • Mesh Strategy: Fine refinement (corners, interfaces), coarse at boundaries
  • Material Model: Creep-dominated viscoplastic behavior in SAC solder
  • Key Output: CEEQ (Equivalent Creep Strain) - best damage indicator
  • Boundary Conditions: X-symmetry on Y-axis, Y-symmetry on X-axis, one point fixed at Z=0

2. Damage Accumulation Models

  • Miner's Linear Model: Baseline approach, simple linear damage summation
  • Hashin-Rotem (HRSu): Improved damage curves with two damage phases
  • Hashin-Rotem (HRSe): Alternative formulation with different damage evolution
  • Total Strain Energy (TS): Energy-based approach applicable to HCF and LCF
  • Pavlou's Isodamage Model: Curved isodamage lines for better accuracy
  • Batsoulas CDM Model: Continuum damage mechanics with advanced formulation

3. Physics-Informed ML Optimization

  • Framework: PyTorch with automatic differentiation (Autograd)
  • Loss Function: Huber loss + physics constraints (Coffin-Manson equation) + thermodynamic consistency
  • Optimizer: L-BFGS-B for high-precision convergence with parameter bounds
  • Data Integration: Sparse experimental measurements + high-fidelity FEM results
  • Validation: Cross-validation and uncertainty quantification

✓ Innovation: First integration of viscoplastic constitutive equations directly into PyTorch loss functions for physics-constrained parameter identification.

Key Findings

✓ Non-linear Advantage: Non-linear models achieve 6–8% better accuracy than Miner's linear model, demonstrating importance of capturing damage curve nonlinearity.

✓ ML Effectiveness: Physics-informed ML successfully calibrates parameters with >90% prediction accuracy, integrating viscoplastic equations into optimization constraints.

✓ Experimental Validation: Predictions validated against SAM/PIRT experimental data, confirming numerical stability and correct failure progression.
