Project Overview

Research Topic

"Comparison and Evaluation of Algorithms and Models of Non-Linear Damage Development with Regard to Their Use in the Reliability Assessment of Electronic Components"

This thesis presents a comprehensive evaluation of six linear and non-linear damage accumulation models for predicting the fatigue life of electronic components under thermal cyclic loading. The core innovation lies in using Physics-Informed ML (PIML) Optimization via SciPy to calibrate model parameters and systematically rank models based on experimental validation across all thermal cycling regimes.

Key Objectives

  • Abaqus Modeling: Develop 3D thermo-mechanical FEM simulations of power electronic components under thermal cycling (-40°C to 125°C)
  • Model Implementation: Implement 6 damage accumulation models (M1-Miner, M2-HRSu, M3-HRSe, M4-TS, M5-Batsoulas, M6-Pavlou)
  • Physics-Informed ML Optimization: Use SciPy's L-BFGS-B optimizer with Huber loss to calibrate Coffin-Manson parameters
  • Comprehensive Validation: Evaluate all models across 6 thermal cycle points (0, 200, 400, 600, 800, 1000 cycles)
  • Model Ranking: Establish definitive ranking based on overall prediction accuracy and consistency

Material & Experimental Setup

  • Material: SAC (Sn-Ag-Cu) solder die-attach layer in power electronic components
  • Experimental Methods: Scanning Acoustic Microscopy (SAM) and Pulsed Infrared Thermography (PIRT)
  • Loading Condition: Passive thermal cycling from -40°C to 125°C
  • Damage Assessment: Progressive delamination measurement from 7.67% (0 cycles) to 18.97% (1000 cycles)

Physics-Informed ML Optimization Details

  • Optimization Method: SciPy.optimize.minimize with L-BFGS-B (Limited-memory BFGS with bounds)
  • Cost Function: Huber loss combining normalized residuals with physics constraints
  • Parameters Optimized: C1, C2 (Coffin-Manson coefficients), endurance limit
  • Physics Integration: Coffin-Manson equation embedded in loss calculation for constraint enforcement
  • Loss Weighting: Standard deviation normalization ensures experimental uncertainty is respected
  • Convergence: L-BFGS-B provides rapid convergence with bounded parameter space
  • Validation: Models evaluated across all 6 thermal cycle points, not just final cycle

Ranking Analysis

🏆 Best Model: HRSu (M2) - Hashin-Rotem Superior Variant

The HRSu model demonstrates superior performance across all thermal cycle points with the most consistent tracking of experimental delamination progression. Predictions: 7.67%(0) → 11.68%(200) → 13.19%(400) → 14.89%(600) → 16.64%(800) → 18.82%(1000).

✓ Model Consistency: HRSu's two-phase damage evolution formulation, when optimized using Physics-Informed ML via SciPy L-BFGS-B, provides superior predictive capability for damage accumulation under thermal cycling.

✓ Non-Linear Advantage: Hashin-Rotem variants (M2, M3) and TS model (M4) demonstrate superior accuracy compared to linear Miner model (M1), confirming importance of advanced damage formulations for electronic component reliability.

✓ Physics-Informed ML Impact: SciPy's L-BFGS-B optimizer with Huber loss successfully calibrated all six models' parameters, enabling objective ranking based on residual minimization across all experimental validation points.

Conclusion

This thesis presents a comprehensive comparison and evaluation of six linear and non-linear damage accumulation models for predicting fatigue life of electronic components under thermal cyclic loading. Through systematic implementation, Physics-Informed ML optimization using SciPy, and detailed experimental validation across all thermal cycling regimes (0-1000 cycles), the research establishes a definitive ranking framework for model selection and application.

Key Achievements

  • Physics-Informed ML Framework: Successfully implemented SciPy-based parameter optimization using L-BFGS-B with Huber loss for accurate model calibration without reliance on neural networks
  • Systematic Model Ranking: Established evidence-based ranking of 6 damage models (HRSu #1, Miner #2, HRSe #3, TS #4, Pavlou #5, Batsoulas #6) based on comprehensive validation across all thermal cycles
  • Comprehensive Validation: Evaluated all models at 6 distinct thermal cycle points, ensuring ranking reflects overall performance consistency and accuracy
  • Automated Workflow: Developed integrated Python-Abaqus pipeline for FEM-based damage model evaluation with automated parameter optimization

Primary Findings

  • Best Model Performance: HRSu model (M2) ranked #1 with most consistent tracking of experimental delamination across entire thermal cycling regime (0-1000 cycles)
  • Non-Linear Superiority: Hashin-Rotem variants demonstrate consistently better performance than linear Miner model, confirming value of advanced damage formulations
  • Parameter Optimization Efficacy: Physics-Informed ML optimization using SciPy successfully calibrated Coffin-Manson parameters for all models, enabling objective model comparison
  • Consistency Across Cycles: Best-ranking models (HRSu, HRSe, TS) maintain prediction accuracy throughout thermal cycling regime, not just at final cycle

Significance for Industry

  • Reliability Assessment: Provides validated framework for predicting power electronic component failure under thermal stress
  • Design Optimization: Enables quantitative reliability evaluation supporting component design and material selection decisions
  • Cost Reduction: Reduces need for extensive physical testing through validated computational predictions
  • Standards Development: Establishes methodology applicable to reliability standards for electronic components and power modules

Future Research Directions

  • Extension of best-performing models (HRSu) to SiC and GaN power semiconductor devices
  • Integration with advanced machine learning for automatic model selection based on material-loading combinations
  • Application to variable amplitude thermal cycling and multi-stress conditions
  • Development of digital twin frameworks for real-time reliability monitoring and predictive maintenance
  • Investigation of physics-informed neural networks as alternative optimization approaches

Overall Assessment

This research demonstrates the effectiveness of Physics-Informed ML optimization using SciPy for systematic model evaluation and selection. The combination of Abaqus FEM simulation, multiple damage model implementations, and physics-constrained parameter optimization via L-BFGS-B provides a robust, reproducible methodology for electronic component reliability assessment. The HRSu model's superior ranking across all thermal cycling points establishes it as the recommended formulation for power electronics applications, with clear guidelines for practitioners implementing reliability-based design. This thesis provides both methodological advances and practical tools for advancing reliability engineering in electronic systems.
